# Standings tiebreakers — help copy

> GameDay's voice. Reworded from SportsEngine's tiebreaker descriptions; safe to use as in-app help (Settings → Standings tiebreakers) and as a reference. Bo to review in detail later.

## How standings tiebreakers work

When teams finish even, GameDay settles it by walking down your tiebreaker list in order. It applies the first rule — if that separates everyone, it's done. If some teams are still tied, it moves to the next rule, and so on.

One important thing: **GameDay never circles back.** Once a rule has been used, it's done with — even if a later rule knocks one of the tied teams out. Say three teams are tied and your first tiebreaker is head-to-head, but they each went 1-1 against one another, so it settles nothing and GameDay moves on. The next rule is goal differential: Team 1 is +3, Team 2 is +2, Team 3 is +1 — so they finish 1, 2, 3. They stay in that order even though Team 2 beat Team 1 head-to-head, because head-to-head was already applied and passed over; it doesn't reopen once Team 3 drops out.

## The tiebreaker options

- **Record** — Mostly winning percentage, but it leans on raw wins (and fewest losses) to break ties. For example, 2-4-0, 1-3-2, and 0-2-4 all share the same winning percentage, but Record ranks them in that order on wins.
- **Winning percentage** — (Wins + ½ × ties) ÷ games played.
- **Points** — If your tournament awards points (e.g. 3 for a win, 1 for a tie, 0 for a loss), this ranks by points earned. A 1-1-1 team has 4; a 0-0-3 team has 3.
- **Head-to-head** — Ranks the tied teams only on how they did against each other (by record, or points if you use them). It only counts when every tied team has played the others the same number of times — otherwise it's skipped — and if they all split evenly, it settles nothing and GameDay moves on.
- **Head-to-head, two teams only** — Same idea, but it only applies when exactly two teams are tied. Three or more tied, it's skipped.
- **Head-to-head goal differential** — Goal differential counting only the games the tied teams played against each other, with the same "equal games played" requirement as head-to-head.
- **Goal differential** — Goals scored minus goals allowed; the biggest margin wins.
- **Goals scored** — Most total goals scored wins.
- **Goals allowed** — Fewest total goals allowed wins.
- **Pool place** — Only for ranking across a whole division: every pool winner outranks every runner-up, who outrank every third-place team, and so on.
- **Goal quotient** — Goals for ÷ goals against; the higher quotient wins. A team that's allowed zero goals (dividing by zero) ranks above any team that's allowed goals; if several teams have zero against, they're ordered by most goals scored.
